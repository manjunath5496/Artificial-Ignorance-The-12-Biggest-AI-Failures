<p><strong>Introduction:</strong></p>
<p><strong>________________________________________________________________________________________________________________</strong></p>
<p>From self-driving cars to industrial robots, all complex real world problems are being solved with applications of intelligence (AI).&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>Artificial intelligence</strong></a>&nbsp;(AI) is progressing rapidly and makes it possible for machines to think like humans and mimic their actions -- adjust to new inputs and perform human-like tasks by processing large amounts of data and recognizing patterns in the data. While<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_fiction" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">&nbsp;<strong>science fiction</strong></a>&nbsp;often renders AI as&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robot" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>robot</strong></a><strong>s</strong>&nbsp;(a machine -- especially one programmable by a&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>computer</strong></a>&nbsp;-- capable of carrying out a complex series of actions without conscious thought or attention) with human-like characteristics, AI can encompass anything from&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Missile" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>missile</strong></a>&nbsp;guidance to&nbsp;<a href="https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tumour" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>tumor</strong></a><strong>&nbsp;detection</strong>&nbsp;to face recognition.</p>
<p>The applications for artificial intelligence are countless and&nbsp;<a href="https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_McCarthy" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>John McCarthy</strong></a>, who coined the term in 1956, defines it as: "the&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">science</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineering" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">engineering</a>&nbsp;of making intelligent machines." The study and design of intelligent agents -- where an intelligent agent is a system that becomes aware or conscious of its environment and takes actions which maximizes its chances of success -- can be applied to many sectors and industries including computer science,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">psychology</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophy" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">philosophy</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuroscience" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">neuroscience</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_science" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">cognitive science</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linguistics" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">linguistics</a>, operations research,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economics" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">economics</a>, control theory,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probability" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">probability</a>, optimization, and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">logic</a>. The simulation of human intelligence in&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">machine</a>s is being tested and used in the maintenance or improvement of health industry for dosing&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drug" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>drug</strong></a>s and different treatment in patients, and for surgical procedures in the hospital operating room.</p>
<p>A property of machines: the intelligence that the system demonstrates -- today is properly known as&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weak_AI" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>Weak Artificial intelligence</strong></a>, in that it is designed to perform a narrow task (such as&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Wide_Web" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">web&nbsp;</a>searches, control systems, scheduling,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_mining" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">data mining</a>, logistics, speech recognition, facial recognition and many others). However, the long-term goal of many technical researchers is to create&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_general_intelligence" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>Strong Artificial intelligence</strong></a>. While Weak Artificial intelligence may outperform humans at whatever its specific task is, like playing games or solving mathematical problems, Strong Artificial intelligence would outsmart humans at nearly every cognitive task.</p>
<p>&nbsp;In little over a decade, Artificial intelligence (a wide-ranging tool that enables people to rethink how we integrate information, analyze&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>data</strong></a>, and use the resulting insights to improve decision making) has made leaps and bounds. Every single day, a new thousand word post showcase the most recent advancement in Artificial intelligence. Being Artificial intelligence has made remarkable breakthroughs, and many scientists dream of creating&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Master_Algorithm" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">the Master Algorithm</a>&nbsp;proposed by&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedro_Domingos" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>Pedro Domingos</strong></a>&nbsp;-- which can solve all problems envisioned by humans -- failure is at the core of human advancement-- notable failures are emerging. From self-driving car accidents to Face ID hacks, AI didn't have a perfect year.</p>

<p>While these are only a few instances of failures that have been observed so far, they are pieces of evidence to the fact that&nbsp;<strong>Artificial intelligence</strong>&nbsp;(the simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems) has the potential to develop a will of its own that may be in conflict with members of the human race. This is definitely a warning about the&nbsp;<strong>potential dangers</strong>&nbsp;of Artificial intelligence which should be addressed while exploring its potential interests.</p>

It therefore follows that computers can, in theory, emulate human intelligence &mdash; and exceed it." &ndash;&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Hawking" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Stephen Hawking</a></p>

<li>&nbsp;Artificial intelligence&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automation" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">automate</a>s repetitive learning and discovery through data.</li>

<li>Artificial intelligence adds intelligence&nbsp;to existing products.</li>
<li>Artificial intelligence adapts through progressive learning&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algorithm" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">algorithm</a>s&nbsp;to let the data do the programming.</li>
<li>Artificial intelligence gets the most out of data.&nbsp;</li>
<li>Artificial intelligence achieves unbelievable accuracy&nbsp;through deep neural networks &ndash; which was previously impossible.&nbsp;For example, your interactions with&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_Alexa" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>Amazon Alexa</strong></a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Search" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>Google Search</strong></a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Photos" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>Google Photos</strong></a>&nbsp;are all based on deep learning &ndash; and they keep getting more precise the more we use them.</li>

<p>&nbsp;The threat of AI-charged job loss is spreading (AI and automation will eliminate the most mundane tasks). No matter what industry you&rsquo;re in, AI-powered bots (which can answer common questions and point users to&nbsp;<strong>FAQs</strong>&nbsp;and knowledge base articles) and software are taking a crack at it. Artificial intelligence seems to be ringing the death knell for all manner of jobs, tasks, chores and activities. From&nbsp;<strong>hospitality</strong>, to&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Customer_service" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><strong>customer service</strong></a>, to home assistants, no job feels safe.
